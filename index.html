The Sun Effect...

Does the weather have an effect or are there other reasons?

By: Sylvain David

Everybody agrees, especially the scientific world, to think there is a correlation between sunlight and depression. In our study based on a data set about the mental health in the tech industry it will be interesting to see if there is a correlation. If not, what would be the explanation. In that situation we will be focusing on the temperature, the income. When we observe the regression between sunlight exposure and count of mental diagnosis by state, we have a negative regression (p-value=0.25). If we take the case of California, which is a state that has 68% of sunlight in a year, the case of mental health diagnosed is high (255). At contrary, New York who have only 46% of days of sun in a year, has “only 92 mental health diagnosed in this study. So, if the sunlight exposure is not really relevant, we should focus on studying if the temperature, the income as an effect on the mental health. In fact, the correlation between average temperature and number of mental health diagnosis is not strong. Even though there is a positive regression, we can’t affirm the temperature has an impact on the mental health diagnosis (p-value=0.80). For example, Florida has a yearly average temperature of 70.70F for35 count of mental health diagnosed. On the other hand, California and Illinois have respectively 59.50F of average for 255 counts and 51.80 of average for 196 counts. If, based on our survey, the weather doesn’t have a significant effect on the mental health, the income has strong effect diagnosis (p-value=0.14). You have more chance to have a mental health concern if your income is higher. Last but not least, is the job sector explain the number of mental health diagnosis. For sure the answer is yes (p-value=0.0001)! The more employees you have the more chance of mental health disorder you have and vice-versa. This is going to explain why California and Illinois are “exception”. They are not outlier but perfect examples for our study.
